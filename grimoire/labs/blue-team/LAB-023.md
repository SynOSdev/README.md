## 📖 Background

A SIEM is the central nervous system of a Security Operations Center (SOC). It collects logs from firewalls, IDS sensors, endpoints, servers, and applications, then normalizes them into a searchable format. Correlation rules combine events from multiple sources to detect complex attacks — such as a brute force followed by lateral movement — that would be invisible to any single sensor.

Key concepts:
- **Log normalization** — Converting diverse log formats into a common schema
- **Correlation rules** — Logic that links events across sources and time to detect attack patterns
- **Sigma rules** — Open-source, vendor-neutral detection signatures
- **Alert fatigue** — Managing the volume and quality of SIEM alerts
- **Retention and compliance** — Storing logs for required durations
- **Threat hunting** — Proactive searching through SIEM data for undetected threats

       .[] |
       {src_ip: .[0].src_ip,

       success:
